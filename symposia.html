Symposia Proposals

This page is structured to support a formal call for symposia, with enough detail to feel credible now while remaining easy to update when final criteria and timelines are approved.

Guidelines

Proposal scope, eligibility, and review expectations

The guidance below is presented as publication-ready placeholder copy covering the main elements organisers typically need before submission.

Eligibility and format

Symposia are intended for organisers who wish to convene a coherent thematic session led by speakers from more than one institution and, wherever possible, more than one country or disciplinary background. Proposed sessions should demonstrate clear scientific rationale, conceptual integration across talks, and relevance to the overall WCNH2027 meeting theme.

As a placeholder format, symposia are expected to run for 75 or 90 minutes and include three to four oral presentations plus time for moderated discussion. Organisers are encouraged to build balance into their panel design, including diversity of career stage, geography, and perspective.

Evaluation criteria

  • Scientific quality, originality, and relevance to the provisional WCNH2027 agenda.
  • Evidence of a coherent session narrative rather than a collection of unrelated talks.
  • Appropriate balance of speakers, institutions, career stages, and methodological perspectives.
  • Clarity of proposed learning outcomes, audience value, and fit within the wider programme.

Proposal requirements

Organisers should prepare a concise symposium abstract that articulates the session's overarching question, explains how the proposed talks connect, and describes why the topic is timely for the conference audience. Individual talk titles and short descriptions should make clear what each speaker contributes to the shared discussion.

Placeholder guidance suggests that each proposal include a nominated chair, a session length preference, and confirmation that proposed speakers have been contacted in advance. Final instructions can also add policies on duplicate submissions, presenter limits, or disclosure requirements if those become necessary.

Deadlines and decisions

Symposia proposals are provisionally scheduled to close on 30 September 2026 [TBC].

Review decisions are planned for late October 2026 [TBC], allowing accepted organisers time to refine session details before abstract submission closes.

Any revisions requested by the scientific committee can be handled through the final submission platform or direct organiser correspondence.
